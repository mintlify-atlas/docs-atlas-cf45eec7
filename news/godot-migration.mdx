On January 30, 2026, Left-Sided Studios announced a major technical shift: every project in the LSS catalog is moving to Godot 4.6. Bumbl, previously built in Unity, and BREAKPOINT, previously built on Unreal Engine 4, will both be ported. Cardamania, which was already in Godot, stays the course as the studio's flagship.

## The discovery

The decision grew out of building Cardamania. Although the game isn't released yet, developing it from the ground up in the Godot Engine gave the team a clear look at how much faster and more cohesive working in a single environment could be.

Before this announcement, LSS projects were spread across three separate engines:

| Project | Previous engine | New engine |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Bumbl | Unity | Godot 4.6 |
| BREAKPOINT | Unreal Engine 4 | Godot 4.6 |
| Cardamania | Godot (already) | Godot 4.6 |

## Why the team is switching

Being a three-person team — Vermetra, RocketBlasts, and DaRealSansYT — means every hour counts. Maintaining separate rendering pipelines and distinct coding environments across Unity, Unreal, and Godot pulled focus away from the games themselves.

Consolidating into Godot 4.6 means the team can share code, shaders, and UI systems across every project. A feature built for Cardamania can be brought into BREAKPOINT with minimal friction. Work compounds instead of being siloed.

> "We believe this move will let us work faster, polish our games harder, and stay true to the 'Left-Sided' philosophy of unconventional, intuitive design."

This is the same philosophy that drives everything at LSS: leveraging the creative, intuitive, and unconventional core of game design to build experiences that respect player agency. Keeping the whole catalog in one engine is a direct expression of that — fewer barriers between an idea and its implementation.
