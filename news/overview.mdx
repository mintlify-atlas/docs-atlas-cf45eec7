Left-Sided Studios publishes development updates, technical blog posts, and studio announcements here. Every post is written by the LSS editorial team — Vermetra, RocketBlasts, and DaRealSansYT — and covers the real decisions shaping our projects.

## Recent posts

### The Godot Leap — January 30, 2026

LSS announced a full engine migration, moving Bumbl (previously Unity) and BREAKPOINT (previously Unreal Engine 4) into Godot 4.6 alongside the already-Godot-native Cardamania. The decision came from the practical reality of a three-person team maintaining separate rendering pipelines. Read the full breakdown of the reasoning, the projects affected, and what this means going forward.

**Tags:** Tech, Development, Godot, Engine

---

### Cardamania release schedule update — November 15, 2025

The studio officially shifted Cardamania's release window to 2028. The core reason: the Commander Engine's multiplayer netcode — which powers Academy Battles — needs networking stability on par with industry standards before launch. This post covers what the delay means for the game and what the team will focus on in the meantime.

**Tags:** Development, Cardamania, TCG

---

### The genesis of Left-Sided — November 10, 2025

The founding story of LSS, tracing back to a summer week in 2023 at Vermetra's house where a Pikmin-inspired concept sparked what would become a multi-label development network. This post covers the origins of the "Left-Sided" philosophy and the early steps that led to the studio's formation.

**Tags:** Studio, Origins, Ecosystem
