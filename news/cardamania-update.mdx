On November 15, 2025, Left-Sided Studios issued an official statement on Cardamania's development timeline. The release window has shifted to 2028. This was not a decision the team made lightly, and the post lays out the specific technical reason behind the change.

## Why 2028?

Cardamania's multiplayer component — Academy Battles — is not a secondary feature. It is the core experience. Players go head-to-head using Commander-style deck strategies, and latency issues would directly undermine the tactical depth the game is built around.

The team's proprietary Commander Engine needs a level of networking stability that matches what players expect from established multiplayer titles. Rushing to an earlier release date would mean shipping an experience that doesn't hold up under real-world multiplayer conditions.

The extended timeline gives LSS the space to:

- Double down on balance testing across all deck configurations
- Harden the multiplayer infrastructure before launch
- Ensure Academy Battles performs reliably at the stability level the game demands

<Note>
  This delay reflects the team's commitment to shipping Cardamania at the quality it deserves. Academy Battles is the heart of the game, and the extra time is being used to make sure it works the way it should.
</Note>
