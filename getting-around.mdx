The Left-Sided Studios web hub is the central portal for everything LSS: original games, subsidiary labels, development news, partner studios, and direct contact with the team. This page explains how the site is structured, how to move between sections, and how to get the most out of features like the global search modal and the built-in AI chatbot assistant.

## Route reference

Every major route in the LSS hub follows a consistent pattern. Use these paths to link directly to any page.

| Route | Content |
|---|---|
| `/` | Home — landing portal |
| `/about` | About — studio info, press kit |
| `/games` | Projects gallery |
| `/games/:id` | Individual project detail (e.g. `/games/cardamania`) |
| `/series/:id` | Media series detail (e.g. `/series/fnaf-otc`) |
| `/network` | Studio network overview |
| `/network/:id` | Subsidiary detail (e.g. `/network/ande`) |
| `/partners/:id` | Partner detail (e.g. `/partners/citadel`) |
| `/news` | News and articles hub |
| `/news/:id` | Individual article (e.g. `/news/godot-migration`) |
| `/contact` | Contact and community links |

## Navbar

The navbar is fixed to the top of every page. On desktop it shows the full link set; on mobile it collapses into a slide-out drawer opened with the hamburger icon.

| Link | Route | Accent color |
|---|---|---|
| Home (LSS logo) | `/` | Emerald |
| About | `/about` | Emerald |
| Projects | `/games` | Emerald |
| Network | `/network` | Purple |
| News | `/news` | Amber |
| Contact | `/contact` | Blue |

The active link is indicated by a colored underline that slides between links. A thin divider separates the nav links from the icon controls on the right.

## Search modal

The global search modal gives you instant access to every piece of content on the site — games, articles, studios, partners, and site pages — from anywhere.

**Opening the modal:** Click the search icon in the navbar or use the keyboard shortcut **Cmd+K** (Mac) / **Ctrl+K** (Windows/Linux).

<Note>
The search modal is accessible on every page. Pressing Escape or clicking outside the overlay closes it without navigating away.
</Note>

### Searchable content types

The modal searches across five content categories, each labeled with a color-coded badge in results.

| Type | Color | What it includes |
|---|---|---|
| Project | Emerald | All games and series from LSS and its subsidiaries |
| Article | Amber | All news posts and blog articles |
| Studio | Purple | LSS parent studio and all subsidiaries |
| Partner | Blue | Strategic and creative partner studios |
| Site | Amber | Main navigation pages (Home, About, Projects, etc.) |

### Filters

The left sidebar of the modal has three filter panels:

- **Content type** — narrows results to one of the five types above, or shows all
- **Status** — filters projects by development status: In Development, Released, Paused, or Canceled
- **Genres** — filters games by genre (e.g. TCG, Platformer, Horror, RPG)

Filters combine with the text query. Selecting a content type and typing a keyword returns only matching items of that type.

### Search prefix commands

Type a prefix word before your search term to jump straight to a content type without using the sidebar filters.

<Steps>
  <Step title="game: or project:">
    Restricts results to Project type. Example: `game: cardamania`
  </Step>
  <Step title="news:, article:, or blog:">
    Restricts results to Article type. Example: `news: godot`
  </Step>
  <Step title="studio: or subsidiary:">
    Restricts results to Studio type. Example: `studio: skullix`
  </Step>
  <Step title="partner:">
    Restricts results to Partner type. Example: `partner: citadel`
  </Step>
  <Step title="site: or page:">
    Restricts results to Site type. Example: `site: contact`
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Prefix commands are case-insensitive. `Game:`, `game:`, and `GAME:` all work the same way.
</Tip>

## AI chatbot assistant

Every page on the LSS hub includes an embedded AI chatbot assistant, powered by Google Gemini. The chatbot appears as a floating button in the lower corner of the screen.

Use it to:

- Ask questions about LSS projects, timelines, and team members
- Get quick answers about studio history and philosophy
- Navigate to a specific page or section by describing what you are looking for
- Learn about the studio's network of subsidiaries and partners

<Note>
The chatbot is contextually aware of LSS content pulled from the site database. It is best suited for factual questions about the studio and its projects rather than general-purpose queries.
</Note>

## Projects gallery

The Projects page at `/games` shows all main LSS titles in a responsive card grid. Each card displays:

- A 16:9 promotional image
- Genre tags
- Development status badge (In Development, Released, Paused, Canceled)

Clicking a card navigates to the project detail page at `/games/:id`. Detail pages include the full game description, a development roadmap with milestone markers, platform info, and links to external pages like GameJolt listings.

<Tip>
Series produced by media subsidiaries (such as Skullix Media Group's cinematic series) are accessible at `/series/:id` rather than `/games/:id`. Both route patterns appear in search results labeled as **Project** type.
</Tip>

## Network and partners

The Network page at `/network` is divided into three sections:

- **Official subsidiaries** — studio units like Endgame Studios and Skullix Media Group
- **Founder imprints** — personal creative labels owned by individual co-founders (ANdE Studios, Gotch-ya Studios, Bomb Banana Studios)
- **Strategic partners** — external studios such as Citadel Studios and Geekza Productions

Each entry links to a detail page at `/network/:id` or `/partners/:id` with a full description, game catalog, and production notes.

## News and articles

The News page at `/news` lists all published blog posts with tag filtering and a keyword search bar. Posts can be filtered by tag (Tech, Development, Announcement, Studio, etc.) or searched by title and excerpt text.

Each article opens at `/news/:id` and may include a related projects section linking back to the games mentioned in the post.
